What you'd reasonably push back on / 04

The questions we usually get.

Four answers, ahead of time. If anything here doesn't land, the walkthrough is the place to dig in.

Q · 01
We already have something for this.
Most flight test programs do — dSpace for HIL, MATLAB and Simulink for analysis, an internal data lake somewhere in between, plus whatever the test card system is. None of that goes away. Nominal sits on top of it as the connective layer: same workbook surfaces HIL output, model predictions, scripts, pipelines, and live flight telemetry on one timeline. The teams we work with kept their stack and gained a shared surface across it.
Q · 02
We're certifying for the FAA and standing up Skyryse Defense.
Then the defense references on this page are operational validation for half the company. Shield AI flies F-16, V-BAT, and MQ-20 through the most regulated test ranges in the world. The Air Force Test Center runs the same review process across Edwards, Eglin, and Arnold. The data discipline that ships unmanned platforms through DoD test authorities is the same one that ships STC artifacts to an ACO: capture, correlate, prove, archive, repeat — every event traceable to a requirement. Different airworthiness authority. Same discipline.
Q · 03
Switching infrastructure mid-program adds risk we can't afford.
Agreed — which is why nobody we work with switched mid-program. Anduril ran Nominal in parallel before they consolidated. Shield AI started on a single airframe before expanding. The right shape for SkyOS is the same: pick one workstream — auto-rotation regression, triplex actuator disagreement analysis, anomaly review on Black Hawk flights — and run it alongside what you have. If it earns the seat, it earns it on its own work.
Q · 04
We're ten years in. Why now?
Because the R66 STC is the first one, not the last. Hardware, operating system, and regulatory artifacts all transfer across airframes — Mark Groden has said as much publicly. The test data discipline that produces those artifacts is the one piece of the stack that usually doesn't. The right time to fix that is before Cirrus, Black Hawk, and fixed-wing are running in parallel — not after.
